# Electric Stair Climbing Wheelchair vs Stairlift: Which Is Right for You?

If stairs are limiting your life, you have two real options: install a **stairlift**, or buy an **electric stair climbing wheelchair** like the XSTO X12 Pro. They solve the same problem in very different ways — and the right answer depends on how you live.

## The Case for a Stairlift

Stairlifts are familiar and (relatively) affordable upfront. A typical UK straight-rail stairlift costs £2,000–£4,000; a curved rail can reach £5,000–£10,000. They install in a day and work for almost any user who can transfer between seats.

**But stairlifts have hard limits:**

- They only work on one staircase, in one home
- You must transfer in and out of your wheelchair each trip
- They add nothing outside the house
- They reduce property value for some buyers
- Power cuts can leave you stranded mid-flight

## The Case for an Electric Stair Climbing Wheelchair

The XSTO X12 Pro is a single mobility device that handles **stairs up to 40°**, kerbs, gravel, grass, ramps, and indoor use. You stay in your chair from morning to night.

**Why owners choose it:**

- Climbs stairs in **any** building — home, friends', shops, workplaces
- 35 km range on dual batteries
- Wheel-track composite chassis with 14 power systems
- IPX5 weather protection — usable in British rain
- 121° recline and electric legrest for all-day comfort
- LiDAR hazard detection prevents drop-offs and collisions
- Travels with you when you move house

## Lifestyle Test

Choose a **stairlift** if you rarely leave the house and only need to access an upstairs bedroom or bathroom.

Choose an **electric stair climbing wheelchair** if you want to stay socially active, visit non-adapted homes, get outdoors, or travel — without ever asking "are there stairs?"
