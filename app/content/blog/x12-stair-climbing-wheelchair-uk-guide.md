# XSTO X12 Stair Climbing Wheelchair UK: Buyer’s Guide

If you are searching for a **stair climbing wheelchair in the UK**, the XSTO X12 series is designed for homes and outdoors where steps, kerbs and uneven ground block a standard power chair. This guide explains who the X12 suits, how it differs from the X12 Pro, and what to check before you buy from the official UK distributor.

## Who the X12 Is For

The X12 is aimed at people who need more than a flat-pavement foldable chair:

- Multi-level homes where a stairlift is impractical or unwanted
- Outdoor routes with kerbs, gravel, grass or gentle rough ground
- Users who want one chair for indoors and outdoors rather than two devices
- Buyers comparing stair climbers against stairlifts or tracked evacuation chairs

If your priority is car-boot portability and light indoor use, start with the [M4 series](/blog/m4-vs-m4-pro-comparison) or [EzGo2](/blog/lightweight-carbon-fibre-wheelchair-ezgo2) instead.

Choose the **X12** when the standard configuration matches your needs. Choose the **X12 Pro** when assessment shows you need specific seating, control or accessory options.

## Practical Checks Before You Buy

1. **Stair geometry** — measure rise, going, width and landings; not every staircase suits every climber
2. **Doorways and turning** — confirm the chair can enter rooms and turn on landings
3. **User weight and posture** — match capacity and seating to the primary user
4. **Storage and charging** — plan a dry indoor charge point and overnight parking space
5. **Training** — stair climbing needs confident practice; book a demo where possible
6. **VAT relief** — eligible disabled customers can often buy VAT-free under HMRC rules

## Stairs vs Stairlift: Quick Context

A stair climbing wheelchair keeps mobility with you — useful if you leave the house often and want one device. A stairlift only solves one staircase. For a deeper comparison, see our guide on [stair climbing wheelchairs vs stairlifts](/blog/electric-stair-climbing-wheelchair-vs-stairlift).
