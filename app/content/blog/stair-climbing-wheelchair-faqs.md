# Stair Climbing Wheelchair FAQs

Considering an **electric stair climbing wheelchair**? These are the questions UK buyers ask us most often, answered using the **XSTO X12 Pro** as the reference model.

## Is a stair climbing wheelchair safe?

Yes. The X12 Pro uses LiDAR to confirm stair geometry before engaging climb mode, gyroscopic self-balancing to keep the seat level, and electromagnetic brakes that hold position instantly if power is interrupted. It refuses to engage on unsafe surfaces.

## What's the steepest staircase it can climb?

Up to **40°**. UK domestic stairs are typically 35°–42°, and most public stairs are shallower, so the X12 Pro handles the vast majority of staircases you'll encounter.

## Can I use it indoors as well as on stairs?

Yes. **Wheeled Quadruped Mode** with four-wheel independent suspension is designed for flat indoor floors, pavements and ramps. You don't need a separate indoor chair.

## How long does the battery last?

Dual 25.2 V 25.6 Ah batteries deliver up to **35 km (22 miles)** of range. Full charge takes around 6.5 hours per battery and you can use one while the other charges.

## How heavy a user can it carry?

Maximum load capacity is **136 kg (300 lbs)**.

## Is it weatherproof?

The X12 Pro is rated **IPX5** — protected against water jets from any direction. It's built for British weather.

## Do I need a carer to operate it?

No. Stair climbing is fully self-operated. Automatic step detection chooses the correct mode and the gyroscope keeps you safely positioned throughout the climb.

## How is it delivered?

Each X12 Pro is built to order with approximately a **12-week** build time. Delivery to mainland UK is free, and a technician handover ensures you're confident with every function.

## What's the difference between the X12 and X12 Pro?

Both share the same chassis, 40° stair climbing, 35 km range and 136 kg load capacity. The **Pro** adds **electric legrest adjustment** and enhanced posture comfort features for users who spend long hours seated.
