# Self-Balancing Wheelchair Technology: How It Works

Traditional wheelchairs can tip on slopes or uneven surfaces. Self-balancing technology solves this problem using the same principles that keep segways upright.

## The Science Behind Self-Balancing

Self-balancing wheelchairs use a combination of:

### Gyroscopic Sensors
Multiple gyroscopes continuously monitor the chair's orientation 1,000+ times per second. They detect even the slightest tilt in any direction.

### Accelerometers
These measure changes in speed and direction, helping the system understand whether movement is intentional or an external force.

### Advanced Algorithms
AI-powered software processes sensor data in real-time, making micro-adjustments to the motors to maintain balance.

### Mecanum Wheels
The XSTO M4 uses omnidirectional mecanum wheels that allow movement in any direction — forward, backward, sideways, and diagonal — while maintaining stability.

## Real-World Benefits

### Slope Navigation
Self-balancing chairs automatically compensate when climbing or descending ramps up to 15°. The seat remains level, keeping you comfortable and secure.

### Uneven Surfaces
Whether it's a bumpy pavement, grass, or gravel, the system constantly adjusts to keep you stable.

### Kerb Negotiation
Many self-balancing chairs can safely mount kerbs that would be impossible for traditional power wheelchairs.

### User Confidence
Perhaps the biggest benefit is psychological — knowing your chair will keep you safe allows you to go places you might otherwise avoid.

## Is It Right for You?

Self-balancing technology is ideal if you:
- Regularly encounter slopes or uneven surfaces
- Value independence and safety
- Want cutting-edge mobility technology

The technology adds some weight and cost compared to basic power wheelchairs, but for many users, the safety and capability benefits far outweigh these factors.
