# VAT Relief for Disabled People: Everything You Need to Know

If you have a long-term illness or disability, you may be entitled to VAT relief on mobility equipment. This can save you 20% on purchases like power wheelchairs.

## Who Qualifies?

You're eligible for VAT relief if you have:
- A physical or mental impairment that has a long-term and substantial adverse effect on your ability to carry out everyday activities
- A condition that requires the use of mobility equipment

**Important:** You don't need to be registered disabled or receive any benefits to qualify. The relief is based on your condition, not your registration status.

## What's Covered?

VAT relief applies to:
- Power wheelchairs and scooters
- Manual wheelchairs
- Hoists and lifts
- Specialist beds
- Stairlifts
- Accessories designed for disability use

## How to Claim

The process is straightforward:

1. **Complete a declaration form** at checkout
2. **Confirm your eligibility** with a brief description of your condition
3. **The seller applies the exemption** — you pay the VAT-exclusive price

No medical evidence is required at point of purchase, but retailers must keep records of declarations for HMRC.

## Common Questions

**Can I claim retrospectively?**
No, VAT relief must be claimed at the time of purchase.

**Does this apply to second-hand equipment?**
Yes, if the seller is VAT registered and the equipment qualifies.

**What if I'm buying for someone else?**
The disabled person must sign the declaration, or it can be signed on their behalf if they're unable to do so.
