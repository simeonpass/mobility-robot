This guide explains how modern stair climbing wheelchairs work, what to look for, and why they're transforming independent living for thousands of UK wheelchair users.

## What Is a Stair Climbing Wheelchair?

A stair climbing wheelchair is a powered mobility device that can ascend and descend stairs under its own power. Unlike a stairlift (which is fixed to one staircase) or a manual stair climber attachment (which requires a trained carer), an **electric stair climbing wheelchair** like the XSTO X12 Pro lets the user climb any staircase, anywhere, on their own.

The X12 Pro uses a dual-track crawler system combined with a wheeled quadruped chassis and **14 integrated power systems** to grip and walk up stair treads safely.

## How the XSTO X12 Pro Climbs Stairs

The X12 Pro switches between three modes automatically:

1. **Wheeled Quadruped Mode** — four-wheel independent suspension for flat ground, pavements and indoor use
2. **Wheel-Track Composite Mode** — engages the rubber tracks for stairs and steep slopes
3. **Track Crawling Mode** — full caterpillar tracks for trenches, gravel, sand and rough terrain

Automatic step detection means the chair recognises when stairs begin and engages the correct mode without the user needing to make any adjustments.

## Who Is It For?

A stair climbing wheelchair is ideal if you:

- Live in a property with stairs and don't want to install a stairlift
