# Foldable Power Wheelchair UK: What to Look For in 2026

A **foldable power wheelchair** can transform daily life in the UK — if it genuinely fits in a car boot, handles kerbs and pavements, and still gives you a full day's range. Many chairs marketed as “portable” are still too heavy for one person to lift. This guide covers what matters for UK buyers, and where the XSTO M4 series and EzGo2 fit.

## Why Foldability Matters in the UK

British homes, trains and car parks reward compact chairs:

- Car boots and hatchbacks vary widely — measure your folded size before you buy
- Flats and narrow hallways need a tight turning circle
- Day trips, hospital visits and holidays are easier when the chair breaks down or folds quickly

## Checklist Before You Buy

1. **Folded dimensions** — check width, height and depth against your boot
2. **Heaviest module** — if the chair dismantles, know the heaviest piece you must lift
3. **Range** — 15 km+ covers most UK daily use; longer days need larger batteries
4. **Slope ability** — look for at least 10° for typical driveways and ramps
5. **VAT relief** — eligible customers can save 20% at checkout under HMRC rules
6. **UK support** — warranty and spare parts handled in the UK, not overseas only

## Travel and Airline Batteries

If you fly, confirm your airline’s lithium battery rules early. Removable batteries help. For air travel, many users prefer a lower-capacity airline-compatible pack (sold separately on some models). Always check with your airline before you book.
