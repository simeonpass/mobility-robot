# Essential Power Wheelchair Maintenance Tips

A well-maintained power wheelchair is a reliable power wheelchair. Follow these tips to keep yours in top condition.

## Daily Checks

### Battery Level
- Check your battery indicator before heading out
- Avoid running batteries to empty — charge when you reach 20-30%
- Deep discharge shortens battery lifespan

### Visual Inspection
- Look for loose screws or components
- Check tyre condition and inflation (if applicable)
- Ensure lights and indicators work

## Weekly Maintenance

### Clean Your Chair
- Wipe down surfaces with a damp cloth
- Use a soft brush to remove debris from wheels and mechanisms
- Avoid pressure washers — electronics don't appreciate them!

### Check Moving Parts
- Listen for unusual sounds during operation
- Test all controls and functions
- Inspect brakes by attempting to push the chair when locked

## Monthly Tasks

### Battery Care
- Clean battery terminals with a dry cloth
- Check connections are secure
- For optimal lifespan, avoid leaving batteries uncharged for extended periods

### Tighten Fasteners
- Go over all visible screws and bolts
- Armrests, footrests, and controller mounts can loosen over time

### Tyre Inspection
- Check tread depth
- Look for cracks, cuts, or embedded objects
- Replace tyres before they become safety hazards

## Seasonal Considerations

### Winter
- Dry your chair thoroughly after wet weather
- Cold affects battery performance — charge indoors
- Check lights work well for shorter days

### Summer
- Don't leave batteries in hot cars
- Clean more frequently if you're outdoors often
- Check for overheating during extended use

## When to Seek Professional Help

Contact your dealer if you notice:
- Significant reduction in range
- Error messages or warning lights
- Unusual sounds or vibrations
- Steering pulling to one side
- Braking issues

Regular professional servicing every 12 months is recommended, even if everything seems fine.
