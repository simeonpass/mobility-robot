# Flying with a Power Wheelchair from the UK: Battery & Travel Checklist

**Flying with a power wheelchair** is doable from UK airports when you plan battery rules, airline notice periods and how the chair will be handled. Airlines treat mobility devices as essential medical equipment — but lithium batteries still follow strict carriage rules. Use this checklist alongside your airline’s latest policy.

## 1. Notify the Airline Early

Contact the airline (and any codeshare partner) as soon as you book:

- Declare you are travelling with a powered wheelchair
- Confirm battery type (usually lithium-ion) and watt-hour (Wh) rating if known
- Ask whether the battery must remain installed, be removed, or travel in the cabin
- Request aisle chairs / assistance for boarding if needed

Keep written confirmation in your phone and printed hand luggage.

## 2. Know Your Battery Basics

Most modern power wheelchairs use **lithium-ion** packs. Airlines and the CAA/IATA framework care about:

- Watt-hour rating (Wh)
- Whether spare batteries are allowed (often cabin only, terminals protected)
- Whether the chair can be accepted as checked mobility equipment

If you are unsure of Wh, check the battery label or ask us before you travel. Never assume a previous flight’s process will repeat on a different airline.

## 3. Prefer Travel-Friendly Chairs When You Fly Often

Frequent flyers often prefer chairs that:

- Fold or dismantle into manageable sections
- Have removable batteries with clear labels
- Come with a soft cover or padding plan for baggage handling

See our [foldable power wheelchair UK guide](/blog/foldable-power-wheelchair-uk-guide) and the ultra-light [EzGo2](/blog/lightweight-carbon-fibre-wheelchair-ezgo2). For day-to-day UK travel without flying, also read [wheelchair-accessible travel in the UK](/blog/wheelchair-accessible-travel-uk).

## 4. Airport Day Checklist

- Arrive earlier than standard assistance recommendations
- Carry charger, essential tools, and spare fuses if your manual advises it
- Photograph the chair from all sides before handover
- Attach a laminated card with your name, flight number and mobile
- Confirm where to collect the chair on arrival (aircraft door vs oversize belt)

## 5. What Families Often Forget

- Hotel accessible rooms and taxi ramps at the destination
- Voltage differences for chargers abroad (UK 230V vs destination)
- Travel insurance that explicitly covers mobility equipment
- A backup mobility plan if the chair is delayed

## 6. VAT Relief Does Not Change Airline Rules

Buying VAT-free in the UK does not alter airline battery policy. Keep purchase and disability documentation for your own records, and follow [VAT relief guidance](/blog/vat-relief-explained) only for the purchase itself.
