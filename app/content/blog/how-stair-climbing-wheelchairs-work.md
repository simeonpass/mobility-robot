# How Stair Climbing Wheelchairs Work: Inside the XSTO X12 Pro

The idea of a wheelchair that climbs stairs sounds futuristic, but the underlying engineering is now mature and certified for everyday use. Here's a clear, jargon-free look at how the **XSTO X12 Pro electric stair climbing wheelchair** actually works.

## 1. Dual-Track Crawler System

At the heart of the X12 Pro is a pair of rubber tracks — like a miniature tank — that deploy from underneath the chassis when stairs are detected. Tracks distribute the user's weight across multiple stair treads at once, providing grip and stability that wheels alone cannot match. The X12 Pro can climb stairs up to **40°** — steeper than typical UK domestic stairs.

## 2. 14 Integrated Power Systems

Most power wheelchairs have two motors. The X12 Pro has **fourteen** coordinated drive and articulation systems controlling wheels, tracks, suspension, recline, legrest and posture. They communicate continuously to keep the seat level and the user safe as the chassis transitions between flat ground and steps.

## 3. Gyroscopic Self-Balancing

Sensors monitor pitch and roll hundreds of times per second. As the chair tips back to climb, the seat rotates forward to keep the user upright and comfortable — the same principle used in self-balancing scooters and humanoid robots, scaled up for safety-critical mobility.

## 4. LiDAR Hazard Detection

Precision **LiDAR** scanning maps the environment in real time. It warns of drop-offs at the top of stairs, detects obstacles, and prevents collisions. If the chair senses unsafe geometry, it refuses to engage stair mode — a critical safeguard.

## 5. Three Modes, Automatic Switching

- **Wheeled Quadruped Mode** — flat ground, indoors, pavements
- **Wheel-Track Composite Mode** — stairs and steep slopes
- **Track Crawling Mode** — trenches, sand, deep gravel

Automatic step detection chooses the right mode without user input.

## 6. Dual Battery Architecture

Two 25.2 V 25.6 Ah batteries deliver up to **35 km of range** and provide redundancy — if one battery is depleted, the other keeps you mobile. IPX5 weather sealing means it works in UK weather.

## 7. Pro Comfort Layer

The X12 Pro adds **electric legrest adjustment** and a 90°–121° recline with anti-fatigue posture cycling. The chair gently shifts your position throughout the day to reduce pressure-sore risk on long sits.

## Is It Safe?

Yes. Stair climbing only engages when LiDAR confirms safe geometry, the gyroscope keeps you upright, and electromagnetic brakes hold position instantly if power is interrupted. Thousands of users worldwide rely on this platform daily.
